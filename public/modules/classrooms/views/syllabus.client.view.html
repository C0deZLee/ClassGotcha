OPTIONAL TEXT: Fox and Cameron’s Food Science, Nutrition, and Health (7th edition, 2006, ISBN 13: 978-1-4441-1337-2) DESCRIPTION & GOALS: This is a general education course (Health and Physical Activity, GHA) that surveys the food system, including: U.S. food policy, food processing and distribution, food components/ingredients, the role of food in health, and scientific research being conducted in these various areas. Though it will be referenced frequently, please note, this is NOT a nutrition class. Current food trends (“fads”), such as organic foods, genetically- modified-organisms (GMOs), nutrition trends, and controversial food ingredients (e.g. Equal/Nutrasweet/Aspartame), will be discussed. With regular attendance and completion of all assigned work, at the end of this course, students should be able to identify: the major components of the food system examples of the legal and economic constraints on foods and the role of governmental agencies in food production and sale 1 techniques used in food processing/manufacture and how they relate to nutritional value ethical, cultural, and individualistic influences on food values and consumption common hazards associated with foods and our role (as consumers) in food safety The semester is roughly broken down into the following parts. In each of these parts, we will discuss current trends related to the food system. Details can be found in the course schedule located at the end of the syllabus. Part 1 – Where does food come from and how is it regulated? Trends discussed: paleo/raw foods diets, “chemicals in food” – sub-topics include Organic agriculture and genetically modified organisms Part 2 – What happens to food prior to arriving in the grocery store? Trends discussed: label claims, processing, and ingredients Part 3 – How does food get to us (consumers) and how do we interact it? Trends discussed: local food, eating behavior & food consumption NOTE TAKING: Notes presented during class will differ from the outlines posted on ANGEL. Baring extenuating circumstances (e.g. hospitalization, death in the family, etc.), there are NO make-up exams. Exams will be administered electronically via the Testing Center.
